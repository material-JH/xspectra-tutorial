# Comparing XSpectra with EELS

Calculated XSpectra outputs and experimental EELS spectra are not automatically on the same energy or intensity scale.

## Alignment

Calculated spectra are usually relative to the electronic structure reference. Experimental spectra use absolute energy loss. For a first tutorial comparison:

1. Normalize intensities.
2. Identify a major experimental peak.
3. Identify the corresponding calculated peak.
4. Apply a rigid energy shift.

## What is meaningful in this tutorial

- approximate peak ordering.
- qualitative changes between no-core-hole, FCH, and HCH.
- O K-edge hybridization features.
- Ti L-edge white-line shape at a qualitative level.

## What is not fully modeled

- plural scattering.
- detailed background subtraction.
- collection/convergence angle dependence.
- sample thickness.
- absolute cross-section intensity.
- full instrumental broadening.

Use the comparison as an interpretation guide, not a final quantitative EELS simulation.
